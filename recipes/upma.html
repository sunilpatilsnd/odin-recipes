Upma

Description

Upma is a beloved South Indian breakfast dish, a savory semolina porridge infused with mustard seeds, onions, green chilies, and curry leaves. Quick to prepare, it offers versatility with optional vegetable additions. Garnished with coriander, it's a flavorful, comforting choice enjoyed with chutney.

Ingredients

  • Semolina (Rava)
  • Oil or Ghee
  • Mustard Seeds
  • Urad Dal (Black Gram Lentils)
  • Chopped Onions
  • Green Chilies
  • Curry Leaves
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Optional: Vegetables (such as peas, carrots, bell peppers)
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat Oil/Ghee: In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee.
  2. Temper Mustard Seeds and Urad Dal: Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and 1 teaspoon of urad dal to the hot oil. Allow them to splutter.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add finely chopped onions (1 medium-sized), green chilies (2-3, chopped), and a few curry leaves. Sauté until the onions become golden brown.
  4. Roast Semolina (Rava): Add 1 cup of semolina (rava) to the mixture. Roast the semolina on low to medium heat until it emits a nutty aroma and turns slightly golden. This step is crucial for flavor.
  5. Boil Water: Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
  6. Gradually Add Water: Slowly pour the hot water into the roasted semolina, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Be cautious as it may splutter.
  7. Season and Simmer: Season with salt to taste. Reduce the heat to low and let the Upma simmer. Cover the pan and cook for 5-7 minutes or until the Upma reaches a porridge-like consistency.
  8. Optional: Add Vegetables: For a nutritious variation, you can add boiled or sautéed vegetables like peas, carrots, or diced bell peppers during the cooking process.
  9. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with coconut chutney, pickle, or yogurt.
  10. Enjoy your delicious and comforting Upma!
